Ahmed Abouelkomsan

I am a postdoctoral researcher in theoretical condensed matter physics at MIT. My research focuses on strongly correlated quantum matter, including fractional Chern insulators, flat bands, moiré materials, Wigner crystals, and unconventional superconductivity.

I am particularly interested in how topology, interactions, and quantum geometry give rise to new phases of matter. I also develop and use computational tools, including exact diagonalization and neural-network variational Monte Carlo.
